# opine — what's left out

Decide these *after* real use, not before.

## Cut on purpose

- **Simulations (debate / policy)** — answer *what happens*, not *what's true*. Peer skill, not a
  move. Move 4 points at the boundary.
- **Delivery / persuasion** — opine forms a view, doesn't land it. A `land-opinion` sibling if wanted.
- **"Restructure to shine light"** — collapsed into Truth + Variations; alone it was reorganizing for
  its own sake.
- **Compromise as a move** — the Frame pivot absorbs the useful half; the rest is negotiation, which
  is delivery.
- **Signal vs noise** — a filter inside Truth, not a step.
- **Prove / disprove** — became the falsifier requirement in Actionables.

## Candidate: pro/anti agent team

Two subagents argue the claim — one for, one against — both briefed that the objective is truth, not
winning, then negotiate toward the path of progress. Would mechanize Move 1 (and feed Variations).

Not built yet, on purpose: it's the fix for a Caution move that comes out weak or lopsided, and we
don't know yet whether it does. Build it when a real run shows Caution failing — that run will also
say whether the team belongs inside opine or is its own skill. Cost is the open question: subagent
fan-out vs. "don't waste my time."

## Watch on first uses

- Frame is heaviest (5 bullets); *audience* and *both-camps* likely merge.
- Does Variations earn separate billing, or fold into Truth?
- Actionables drifting into a list instead of a ranking by cheapest-per-unit-of-truth.
- The refine-idea boundary (claim vs. project) is asserted, untested.
- Tone under a genuinely bad opinion — does it stay collaborative or revert to prosecuting?

## Not done

- **No evals** — outputs are judgment calls; assertions would measure the wrong thing.
- **No description optimization** — n/a while `disable-model-invocation: true`.
